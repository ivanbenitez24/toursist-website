Island Garden City of Samal(Samal City, Davao del Norte)

Samal Island is also known as the "Island of Festivals" because it celebrates several events such as the Hugyaw Madayaw every August, the Kabasan Festival, a week-long revelry celebrated every month of May, and the Caracoles Festival held during April.

Maria Cristina Falls(Iligan City, Lanao del Norte)

Iligan City, located in the province of Lanao Del Norte, is not called City of Majestic Waterfalls for no reason. The city got its nickname because of the presence of numerous waterfalls in it. The local government has listed 24 waterfalls in Iligan and the most prominent of all is the Maria Cristina Falls. This waterfall is sometimes called the “twin falls” because of the large diving the waterfall into two.

Hinatuan Enchanted River(Hinatuan, Surigao del Sur)

This mystical site marked by its shimmering, crystal clear blue waters, surrounded by thick jungle, vines, and orchids, has, therefore, become one of the premier tourist destinations of the province. Other than its beauty, there are tales of mermaids, magical fairies and spirits at the Hinatuan Enchanted River as well.

Mount Apo(Davao)

Mount Apo, also known locally as Apo Sandawa, is a large solfataric, dormant stratovolcano on the island of Mindanao, Philippines. With an elevation of 2,954 meters above sea level, it is the highest-mountain in the Philippine Archipelago, Mindanao and 24th-highest peak of an island on Earth.
